Ether price vs Ethereum Gas price - more utility in the Ethereum network?

2022/03/26

crypto analysis |

Comparison of Ether and Ethereum Gas prices

Objective

The aim of this article was to compare the prices of Ether and Ethereum Gas. Additionally an attempt was made at answering a question of whether Ethereum network is gaining more utility.

Methodology

The main method used in this article is visual presentation and discussion. A program was written using python and jupyter lab environment to collect the relevant data and project it onto a graph.


Fig. 1. Python code for Ether and Ethereum Gas prices comparison Source: Own study


Time range

Time period between 2015 and 2022.

Results

Fig. 2. Graph presenting comparison of Ether and Ethereum gas prices - short term Source: Own study


Fig. 3. Graph presenting comparison of Ether and Ethereum gas prices - long term Source: Own study

Recent years have seen increase in Ethereum gas prices compared to early years of the Ethereum protocol history.


Fig. 4. Graph presenting the biggest consumers of Ethereum gas Source: Etherscan.io

OpenSea has been in the highest positions for the longest time as a major Gas guzzler, and the same is true for Uniswap. This indicates that both NFTs and Defi have been the biggest factor behind high gas prices in recent years.

Conclusion

In recent months there has been a drop in gas prices, mainly due to the market downturn as a whole. However, in recent years the gas prices have exploded as a result of great interest in NFTs and Defi. The NFT hype has contributiled a lot to the high gas price, also because NFT transactions are amongst the most expensive ones. This also indicates that the Ethereum network is gaining more utility than just simple token exchange and might be a prelude to some wider structural market changes.

As can by seen on a log scale showing the price of Ether, in 2016 the increase in Ether price was much stronger then in 2020, yet the price of gas stayed relatively low. This correlates well in time with the emergence of popularity of NFTs and Defi.

Generally speaking, in most cases the dip in price was also followed by a dip in ethereum gas price, it wasn’t, however, always like that. Some exeptions like the one in September 2020 can be observed on the short term graph (Fig 1). In that case the price of Ether was increasing whereas the gas price continued to dip right into the following year.

Trade in NFTs is one of the factors behing substantial increase in gas prices in recent years compared to earlier periods.
