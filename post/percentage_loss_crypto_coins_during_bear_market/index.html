Percentage loss from peak to bottom for crypto coins during 2022 bear market

2022/05/22

crypto analysis |

Crypto is in a bear market. How did individual coins fare compared to one another?

Introduction

The crypto market is currently in a bear market (second quarter of 2022). Some analysts expect it to fall even lower, and according to others, the market has already hit the bottom.

As every crypto investor knows, the crypto market is not a monolith. It consists of many protocols with different utility. So at least in theory it should exhibit varied performance between different coins.

In the current crypto bear market, most coins have fallen down in value. Some of the stable coins are an exception, but not all of them.

TerraUSD for instance, became decoupled from the dollar, and on the date of writing is trading at 0.057 USD, which is a fraction of its original value.


Some of the so called reserve currencies such as Wonderland on Avax were rug pulled. Other example such as non pegged stablecoin Olympus (OHM), lost a lot of value in this bear market. It can be argued however, that the value of its token is not supposed to be pegged to any fiat currency, but is meant to float based on the value of its underlying treasury of assets and parameters set by the DAO. So in essence that was an expected behavior.

There is no doubt however, that most coins have lost value due to the recent slump.

Objective

The aim of this article is to compare percentage loss between several of the better known cryptocurrencies.

Time period

The analysis looks at prices of selected cryptocurrences in time period between 11.08.2021 and 21.05.2022

Methodology

A number of cryptocurrencies were considered for analysis based on availability of data within required time span, and their coingecko marketcap value. In the end 52 most popular coins were selected. Price data was then downloaded from available sources. After data cleaning, the number of coins for analysis was finally reduced to 49.

A python program was written in order to automate the analysis.


Fig. 1. Python source code for analysis of percentage loss of crypto currencies during current bear market Source: Own study


Results

The outcome of the analysis is presented in a bar chart. The horizontal red line represents the mean value which stands at 78%. Meaning that most cryptocurrencies lost around 78% of their value up to date.


Fig. 2. Percentage loss of crypto currencies during the current bear market Source: Own study


Best vs Worst Performers Cryptocurrencies
Best 1. Monero, 2. Tron, 3. Bitcoin
Worst 1. Luna, 2. Fantom, 3. Sushi

Looking at the chart, it can be seen that despite falling 50%, Monero had the best performance among all the analyzed cryptocurrences. What are the reasons for such behavior?

According to discussions on dedicated Monero threads, some of the advantages of Monero include:

  • Less moonboys - attracts mainly privacy conscious individuals.
  • More utility then BTC - once you pay someone, nobody can see your other transactions or your wallet contents.
  • Strong and dedicated community, consisting of hackers and activists, stronger hands.
  • Lower barrier to entry in terms of mining. The protocol is designed to be mined on CPU rather than GPU.

Conclusion

Despite the fact that almost all crypto coins experienced almost 80% retreat from the peak, some of them fared better then others. Monero (XMR) is a clear winner in this case. Terra Luna is the biggest loser so far with 100% retreat, and the case is regarded by some as rug pull.

Most crypto currencies retreated on average 78% from the top. For stock market this would be considered a disaster. But for the volatile crypto markets, this seems to be business as usual.
