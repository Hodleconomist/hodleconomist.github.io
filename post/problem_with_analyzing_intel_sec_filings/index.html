The Problem with Intel’s SEC filings and Natural Language Processing

Objective

The aim of this article was to show how chipmaker Intel Corporation makes use of their own 10-K form which differs substantially from the SEC 10-K, making automation of document reading much more difficult for analysts.

Methodology

The main method used in this article is visual presentation and discussion.

Results

In its latest 10-K SEC filing, Intel Corporation states as follows:

Organization of Our Form 10-K The order and presentation of content in our Form 10-K differs from the traditional SEC Form 10-K format. Our format is designed to improve readability and better present how we organize and manage our business. See “Form 10-K Cross-Reference Index” within the Financial Statements and Supplemental Details for a cross-reference index to the traditional SEC Form 10-K format. We have defined certain terms and abbreviations used throughout our Form 10-K in “Key Terms” within the Financial Statements and Supplemental Details.


Meanwhile, the Securities and Exchange Commission has adopted amendments that require companies to provide their financial statements in interactive data format using eXtensible Business Reporting Language (XBRL) by submitting them to the SEC and posting them on their corporate Web sites.

The SEC states that the purpose is the following: The primary purpose of the amendments is to make financial information easier for investors to analyze and to assist companies in automating regulatory filings and business information processing .

The XBRL a global not for profit organization with the purpose to improve the accountability and transparency of business performance globally, by providing the open data exchange standard for business reporting, stated the following: Itâ€™s great to see that the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is doubling down on its commitment to digital disclosures across a wide area.


Not only Intel does not provide its business description, and many other parts of the report in XBRL format, but not even HTML or text format was used. Intel provides parts of the information for 10-K filings as images making it brochure like publication. This brakes down many automation programs, as the SEC 10-K format isn’t followed and makes it much more difficult to analyze text with Natural Language Processing methods, since text extraction from images must first be used.



Fig. 1. Intel’s custom 10-K table of contents Source: SEC filings


Intel’s custom 10-K table of contents looks much different then the standard 10-K SEC form. Intel claims that it aims at increased readability. This however changes the format and breaks many programs designed for business information processing and contradicts streamlining of forms which SEC wants to make.


Fig. 2. Intel’s SEC format conforming index, located as the final item of the report Source: SEC filings


Intel put the SEC conforming cross-reference index as the final item in the report. Items conforming to the SEC format are spread around hundreds of pages.


Fig. 3. Intel’s brochure like images as 10-K SEC filings Source: SEC filings


Some parts of Intel’s 10-K SEC filings which include discussion are presented as jpg images.


Conclusion

The Intel’s 10-K SEC definitely does not improve on readability, and certainly spoils the well known structure of the document which many investors rely on. Intel’s biggest competitors like Nvidia and AMD, as well as, many other semiconductor companies, conform to the standard SEC 10-K filing format. It looks like Intel want’s to channel reader’s attention into its own agenda.

In our opinion, if Intel wants to provide financial content “designed to improve readability” for legacy investors, they can as well publish the brochure as another file on their website, or even send them out physically to their shareholders like it used to be in the 90'. The SEC 10-K reports are meant to be for professional analysts and interest holders and should always conform to the standard SEC format.

Intel Corporation SEC filings totally miss their intended application and audience.
