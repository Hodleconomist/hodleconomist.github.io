Overview of revenues and profits of Pharmaceutical and Biotech industries with Python and SQL - part 1

2022/06/16

stock analysis |

How does Pharmaceutical industry compares to Biotech industry in terms of yearly revenue and profit?

Introduction

How to define the Pharmaceutical and Biotech industries? According to Investopedia Biotechnology and Pharmaceutical companies both produce medicines. However, the medicines made by Biotechnology companies are derived from living organisms while those made by Pharmaceutical companies generally have a chemical basis. In particular, Biotechnology harnesses cellular and biomolecular processes to develop technologies and products. In reality it isn’t that straight and simple, since many traditional big Pharmaceutical companies already produce drugs based on living organisms, or have acquired Biotech companies to produce drugs in multiple technologies.

This is the first of a series of articles concentrating on Pharma and Biotech industry. In this article we try to compare revenues and profits of both industries within the span of several years.

The analysis focuses only on industries consisting of companies which conform to the SEC 10-K filing requirement and does not include privately held entities.

Objective

The aim of this article is to examine and compare two industries, the Pharmaceutical and Biotech industry in terms of revenue and profit over several years and show the process of collecting and analyzing data using both Python Pandas Dataframe and SQL queries.

Time period

The analysis uses data from 2012 to 2022

Methodology

Description

First step in the analysis was to determine which publicly traded companies fall into Pharmaceutical and Biotechnology category. A screening service from Finviz website was used for that purpose. Finviz.com provides company screener which allows for simple determination of sectors and industries. Yearly financial information such as income statement and balance sheet was collected using the Stockrow website by utilizing the site API.


The process of collecting data for each company was the following:

  1. Collection of yearly income statement and balance sheet data in excel format from Stockrow
  2. Load excel sheets into Pandas Dataframe
  3. Automatic data cleaning consisting of elimination of NaN values, checking for non-existing columns, fill-in empty gaps, other.
  4. Combination of income statement and balance sheet into a single Dataframe
  5. Upload of combined Dataframe to Sqlite database

This process managed to save disk space and speed up the retrieval of data by delegating some tasks directly to the database.


The second step consisted of querying the database in search for a number of financial items, such as:

  1. Number of companies for each industry
  2. Total revenue per year for each industry
  3. Total net income per year for each industry
  4. Average revenue per year for each industry

In order to automate this process, a python program and SQL queries were prepared.

All tables show the increase in the number of companies and revenues for both industries from 2021 up to 2021 with the highest revenue reported in most recent year. Average revenue is also in an upward trend for both Biotech and Pharma.

The comparison of tables 2 and 3 shows that in 2020 revenue of 20 big Pharmaceutical manufacturers was almost 10 times higher than that of 536 Biotechnology companies, excluding those startups that did not report any sales.

Year 2021 was different due to Covid-19, where a two Biotech companies working on mRNA technology managed to capture more than half the sales of the rest of industry.

It’s also worth mentioning that in 2021, more than 220 Biotech companies went without any revenues, existing with external funding from investors betting on future revenue streams from current R&D efforts.

Comparison of profits between the Pharma and Biotech industries proves that the later mainly consists of cost incurring companies which frequently report losses. Each year, starting from 2012, the negative profits have been growing in value for the Biotech industry. This is due to the growing number of new startup companies being listed on exchanges, as well as, investors willingness to risk capital on new drug discoveries.

Year 2021 was an exception to the growing losses of Biotech industry, mainly due to the large profits of mRNA companies.


Conclusion

The Biotech industry includes significantly more companies and proves to be much more fragmented then traditional big Pharma manufacturers. Many Biotech companies frequently report losses and do not show any revenues.

This results from the fact that many Biotech companies are set up as research and development startups with external financing in search of new drug discoveries.

Another interesting conclusion is that in 2012 only 11% of the total number of Biotech companies did not show any revenues, whereas in 2021 this percentage grew to 42%.

Biotech industry frequently report losses and no revenues. In 2021 almost 41% Biotech companies went without revenues, compared to only 11% in 2012.
