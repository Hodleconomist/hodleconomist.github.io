What is DAO?

DAO - Decentralized Autonomous Organization is an organization operating in accordance with the rules encoded as a computer program also known as a smart contract (smart contract). Think of it as a kind of company, registerd on blockchain that is run by smart contracts.

What is a smart contract? This is a self-executing contract where the terms between buyer and seller are written directly into the lines of code. The code and the contracts it contains exist on a distributed, decentralized blockchain network.

DAO is computer code - in the form of a smart contract and operating on the Blockchain network

DAO is nothing more than a computer algorithm that implements property rights, contractual obligations and the rules of business logic. When all these things are connected, we get an autonomous, data-driven, transparent company managed by smart contracts that distribute the generated value among its shareholders (in this case token holders).

The goal of DAO is to create an organization that can function without the human factor and the associated hierarchical management.

Now let’s look at some details:

As a rule, any interaction between people and organizations can be expressed as a contract. Smart contracts built on the basis of blockchain technology allow the creation of such organizations based on the cloud. Thus, DAO makes it possible to automate all management and administrative functions in the organization.

DAO is not only a technological revolution. In conjunction with artificial intelligence, DAO could create its own products and services, and select the appropriate advertising campaigns to maximize sales, while the profits would go to individuals with shares in DAO (token holders).

What are the biggest obstacles to the large-scale introduction of DAO today? Apart from some technical complexities, the solution of which is still at the research stage, the biggest barrier to a wider adaptation of this model is the fact that from a legal point of view, there is no legal classification or recognition of DAO yet.

Nevertheless, there is a good chance that these data-driven and fully automated organizations will become the main threat to most traditional organizations in the coming years.
